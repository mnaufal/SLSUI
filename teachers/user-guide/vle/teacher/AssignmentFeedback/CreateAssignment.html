Create an Assignment (New)

You can create an Assignment by assigning a Lesson or Course to students in your Class Groups.

Starting from R17, you can turn your Assignments into Assessments. Assessment Cards are differentiated with a beige background.

Note: For better user experience, multiple assignments will be created in groups of 100 students if lessons are assigned to more than 100 students.

Assign a Lesson or Course

  1. You can access My Drive by opening the left menu, under Resources.

  2. Click Open under the Lesson or Course title.

  3. On the Lesson or Course page, click the Assign icon found on the top right of the header.

  4. On the Assign Lesson or Course subpage, you will see all the Class Groups that are assigned to you. Class Groups that you have created can be found there too. Select the Class Groups you would like to assign the Assignment to, then click Assign.

  5. Alternatively, from the Class Group page, click Add Assignment to assign a Lesson or Course.

  6. On the Assignment Title subpage, you may edit the Assignment Title and Permission Settings.

  7. You may also pause the Assignment upon assigning by checking the relevant checkbox. Click Next to proceed.

  8. You may also adjust the Assignment Permissions. These Permission settings will not affect your current Lesson/Course in your My Drive, but subsequent copies of this Assignment.

  9. On the Assignment Summary page, you will see an Assignment Card for the selected Class Group(s). Each Class Group's Assignment Settings can be modified by clicking the Pencil icon to access the Assignment Settings subpage.

  10. You may modify the following in the Assignment Settings subpage:

    • Students Assigned - add or remove students by checking or unchecking the boxes next to the students' names. You may select students by Class Group or Subgroup by clicking the dropdown arrow to show the students' names according to their index number.

      Note: Students who appear in more than one assigned Subgroup will only be assigned the Assignment once. Students who are in more than one of the assigned Class Groups will receive the assigned Lesson or Course multiple times (i.e. Students will receive the Assignment once for each Class Group assigned).

    • Schedule - change Assignment Start and Due Date/Time. You can choose to send students a notification when the Assignment has started by selecting the checkbox beside Notify students assigned when assignment starts.

      Note: You can change the Start and Due Dates of individual Sections within a Course.

    • Activities / Teams (New) - change the accessibility to Activities and manage Teams. Students will not be able to access the activity if the Student Access toggle is switched off.

    • Gamification Settings (Only for Courses with Gamification toggled on) - Allow students to view individual or team leaderboards in a gamified Course by selecting the checkbox beside the respective options. You can also edit Game Teams.

    • Others:

      • Assessment Settings - Turn the Assignment into an Assessment by selecting the checkbox beside Set as assessment.

      • Student-Sharing Permissions - Grant permission to students to share their Lesson or Course by selecting the checkbox beside Allow students to share lesson or course.

  11. If you want to assign an Assignment with a common Assignment Setting to more than one Class Group, click Common Assignment Settings. You can set a common Assignment Schedule, Assessment Settings, or Permissions.

    Note: By setting Common Assignment Settings, it will overwrite any existing settings that were previously set.

  12. Click Save to save your changes and X to close the subpage.

  13. Click Assign, found on the top right of the Assignment Summary page to assign the Lesson or Course.

    14. Note: For Courses with multiple Sections, it may take some time for the Assignment to appear on your Assignment List.

DID YOU KNOW?

Starting from R18, Teams will replace the previous “Groupwork” function. You can now create different Teams for the different Activities or use Subgroups as Teams.
